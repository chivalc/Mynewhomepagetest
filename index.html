A bit about me.

I am Chiedu, married with children and of African Heritage.

I have a couple of hobbies but the ones that stand out the most are learning, technology, fast cars though I do not have one yet.

From an early age I have been interested in information technology. I recall the feeling of wanting to know how computers worked, why they worked and what else they could do. After graduating from secondary school, I sought employment in a document support centre using Microsoft office tools, Corel Draw, AutoCad and the likes to prepare and beautify documents.

This interest never left me, rather, it grew more profound, and I became more passionate with every new discovery I made - from communicating with artificial intelligence to seeing the wonders of the internet for the first time. Technology has left me fascinated with just how much power yet mystery they hold.

My interest in computing has never been restricted. I have used the knowledge that I have gained over the past years and with support from friends and family to start delving into the information technology world properly and I am hopeful that I will be gainfully employed and possibly set up my own technology related business in the future. The more information I find gives me new perspective on how certain things function.

Enrolling into the Code Your Future Programme has been a great opportunity for rediscovery. Coding was a totally alien experience for me, but over the last weeks, since the start of the Code Your Future Programme application process, I have done a lot of research and planned, and finally approaching the completion of my application process I am beginning to feel the satisfaction of knowing that I have the rare opportunity that most people would wish they had.

To ensure that I can fit everything in my personal life, part time job and this new developmental endeavour, I have had to develop very good time management and prioritising skills.

I have enjoyed enormous support so far from Code Your Future trainers and I like the idea of being part of a supportive family engaging with people who accept and support one another in challenging and successful times.

I like the idea of creating long-term, measurable impact in society which I am now a beneficiary of. In the future I will like to contribute in helping people make tangible, positive changes in their lives by improving their social mobility and reducing inequality. I believe in community and giving back, and I am hopeful that someday I will be able to contribute my quota to the prosperity of the Code Your Future Programme and the future cohort that will enrol into the program. I will also spread the good news of CYF to all that are interested.

I am looking forward with great anticipation to the challenges that studying with Code Your Future will bring.
